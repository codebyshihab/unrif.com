The Birth of UNRIF: An Inspiring Journey

The Ullal Non-Resident Indian Forum (UNRIF) was an initiative started by Ullalians in KSA (Kingdom of Saudi Arabia). What began as a casual discussion during a friendly cricket match with friends from Ullal soon transformed into a mission to address key community needs. With the formation of a WhatsApp group, the vision took shape, and the groundwork for this impactful organization was laid.

This organization holds significant importance for the Ullal zone, as it aims to address critical areas that directly impact the lives of the residents. By focusing on education, health, and empowerment, UNRIF aspires to build a stronger, more united community where every individual can thrive.

The UNRIF core committee has outlined its initial focus areas, covering North Ullal (Kallapu) to South Ullal (Kotekar) and East Ullal (Kuttar) to West Ullal (entire Ullal coastal region). This geographic scope reflects the forum's commitment to ensuring that its efforts resonate with the heart of the community. Future plans include expanding the forum's reach to encompass the entire Ullal constituency, making the initiative even more inclusive.

The Main Agendas of UNRIF

Education:

Empowering youth and adults through accessible educational programs and skill-building opportunities.

Health:

Promoting health awareness and improving access to healthcare services for all residents.

Empowerment:

Supporting initiatives that uplift individuals and communities through leadership, employment, and social programs.

Together, with the active participation of Ullal residents and well-wishers, UNRIF is poised to make a lasting difference in the region.
